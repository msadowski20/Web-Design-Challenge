Humidity vs. Latitude

This graph looks at the relationship between the humidity of a city and its proximity to the equator (latitude 0). It shows that there is not much of a correlation between latitude and humidity. There are areas of very high and very low humidity both close to, and far away from the equator.

We also compared the Northern and Southern Hemispheres and performed a linear regression on the findings. There does not seem to be a very strong correlation between humidity and latitude. However, there does seem to be a slight positive correlation in the Northern Hemisphere and a slight negative correlation in the Southern Hemisphere; meaning in the Northern Hemisphere as you get further away from the equator humidity increases, and in the Southern Hemisphere it decreases as you get further away from the equator.
